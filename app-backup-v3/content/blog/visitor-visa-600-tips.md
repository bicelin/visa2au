The Visitor Visa (subclass 600) is one of the most commonly applied-for Australian visas. Whether you are visiting for tourism, business, or to see family, these tips will help strengthen your application.

## 1. Demonstrate Genuine Temporary Entrant Intent

The most critical factor in a visitor visa application is convincing the case officer that you genuinely intend to visit temporarily. Provide evidence of:
- Strong ties to your home country (employment, property, family)
- A clear reason for your visit with supporting documents
- Evidence of sufficient funds for your stay

## 2. Provide Comprehensive Financial Evidence

You need to demonstrate that you can support yourself during your stay. Bank statements, payslips, and evidence of assets are essential. If someone is sponsoring your visit, include their financial documents and a formal invitation letter.

## 3. Include a Detailed Itinerary

A well-planned itinerary shows you have thought through your visit. Include flight bookings (or quotes), accommodation details, and planned activities.

## 4. Address Health and Character Requirements

Depending on your circumstances, you may need to undergo health examinations or provide police clearances. Check the requirements early and allow time for these.

## 5. Be Honest and Thorough

Inconsistencies or missing information can lead to delays or refusal. Double-check all information before submitting and ensure your supporting documents are clear and relevant.

## Common Reasons for Refusal

- Insufficient evidence of ties to home country
- Inconsistent information across documents
- Concerns about financial capacity
- Previous visa compliance issues
