Australian citizenship represents the final milestone in your migration journey. As a citizen, you gain the right to vote, apply for an Australian passport, and enjoy the full protection of the Australian government.

## Pathways to Citizenship

**Citizenship by Conferral (most common)**
This is the pathway for permanent residents who have lived in Australia for a specified period and meet all eligibility requirements.

**Citizenship by Descent**
For people born outside Australia to at least one parent who was an Australian citizen at the time of their birth.

## Eligibility Requirements

- Be a permanent resident at the time of application and decision
- Have lived in Australia for at least 4 years (including 1 year as a permanent resident)
- Have been physically present in Australia for at least 12 months of the past 2 years
- Be of good character
- Pass the citizenship test (if aged 18-59)
- Intend to reside in or maintain a close connection with Australia

## The Citizenship Test

The test assesses your knowledge of Australia and the responsibilities and privileges of citizenship. It covers:
- Australian values
- Australian history and culture
- Government and law in Australia

## Processing and Ceremony

After a successful application, you will be invited to attend a citizenship ceremony where you make the Australian Citizenship Pledge and receive your certificate.
