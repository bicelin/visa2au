# Urgent visa help — s56 requests, refusals and cancellations

> Received an s56 request, visa refusal or cancellation notice? Response deadlines are strict (7–28 days) and cannot be extended. What to do immediately — and how MARN-registered agents can help.

## Response deadlines
- s56 Request for More Information: Usually 28 days. The exact date is in your letter — it cannot be extended. A late or weak response usually means refusal.
- Visa refusal — ART appeal (most visas): 21 days. From the date of decision. The Administrative Review Tribunal cannot extend this limit.
- Protection visa (866) refusal: 7 working days. The shortest deadline in the system. Contact an agent the day you receive the decision.
- Visa cancellation / NOICC: Days, not weeks. Response windows to a Notice of Intention to Consider Cancellation are very short. Immediate advice essential.

## Situations
### s56 — Request for More Information
The Department is asking for additional documents or explanation before deciding your application. This is a critical opportunity — not a formality.
What we do: Do not rush a partial reply. We review exactly what the case officer is signalling, gather the right evidence, and respond once — completely and on time.

### Visa refusal — ART merits review
A refusal is often not the end. The Administrative Review Tribunal (ART) independently re-examines the decision — many refusals are set aside on review.
What we do: Lodge the appeal within the strict limit (21 days; 7 working days for protection), then build the case properly: new evidence, submissions, hearing preparation.

### Visa cancellation / NOICC
Your existing visa is at risk — over conditions, character grounds, or information provided. A Notice of Intention to Consider Cancellation starts a very short clock.
What we do: Immediate professional response. What you say in the first reply shapes everything after. Do not respond without advice.

## Immediate steps
1. Read the letter carefully — Identify exactly what is being asked, and note the deadline date — it is fixed and cannot be extended.
2. Stop and don't improvise — No rushed replies, no new applications, no travel bookings until you have advice. A wrong move can close options.
3. Gather your documents — The decision letter or request, your current visa grant notice, passport, and any evidence you hold.
4. Contact a registered agent — fast — Within 1–7 days. Call or email with "URGENT" in the subject and attach the letter.
