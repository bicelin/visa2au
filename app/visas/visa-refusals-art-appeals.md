# Visa Refusals & ART Appeals (subclass ART review) — Visa2AU

> Refused, cancelled, or facing a deadline? Act fast. Category: Other.

The Administrative Review Tribunal (ART, formerly AAT) can review most visa refusal and cancellation decisions — but strict deadlines apply, often 21–28 days from notification. Early case analysis is the difference between a winnable review and a lost right.

## At a glance
- Government charge: ART fee: AUD 3,496 (refundable 50% if successful, exemptions exist)
- Indicative professional fee: $5,500 AUD (Honest prospects assessment before you commit)
- Typical processing: Deadlines: usually 21–28 days to lodge · Review: 6–18 months

## Eligibility
- A reviewable decision (most refusals/cancellations)
- Lodgement within the strict time limit
- Usually onshore with a bridging visa during review

## Key documents
- The refusal/cancellation decision record
- Complete original application file
- New evidence addressing the refusal reasons
- Submissions responding to each refusal ground

## Frequently asked questions
### Is every refusal worth appealing?
No. We first obtain and analyse the decision record: some refusals turn on fixable evidence gaps, others on findings unlikely to change. You get an honest prospects assessment and a fixed quote before deciding.
