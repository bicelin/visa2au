# Partner Visas (subclass 820/801) — Visa2AU

> For partners of Australian citizens, PR holders or eligible NZ citizens. Category: Family.

The onshore partner visa lets the spouse or de facto partner of an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen live in Australia. It is a two-stage pathway: temporary (820) then permanent (801), usually assessed from a single application.

## At a glance
- Government charge: From AUD 11,710 (main applicant)
- Indicative professional fee: $5,500 AUD (Fixed professional fee quoted after assessment)
- Typical processing: 12–24 months (typical)

## Eligibility
- Genuine and continuing spouse or de facto relationship (12+ months or registered)
- Sponsor is an Australian citizen, PR or eligible NZ citizen
- Onshore at time of application (820/801 stream)
- Meet health and character requirements
- Sponsor meets character and sponsorship obligations

## Key documents
- Identity documents (passports, birth certificates)
- Relationship evidence: joint finances, lease/mortgage, bills
- Social evidence: photos, invitations, travel together
- Statutory declarations (Form 888) from witnesses
- Police clearances and health examinations

## Frequently asked questions
### How long does a partner visa 820/801 take?
Most onshore partner applications are processed in 12–24 months for the temporary 820 stage. The permanent 801 is usually assessed around two years after lodgement, provided the relationship is ongoing.

### Can I work while my 820 visa is processed?
Yes. The bridging visa granted with an onshore 820 application generally comes with full work rights once your current visa ceases.

### What evidence is strongest for a partner visa?
Decision-makers weight evidence across four aspects: financial interdependence, the nature of the household, social recognition of the relationship, and mutual commitment. Consistent evidence over time beats volume.
