# Prospective Marriage Visa (subclass 300) — Visa2AU

> Come to Australia to marry your fiancé(e). Category: Family.

The subclass 300 visa allows an engaged partner to enter Australia, marry their Australian sponsor within the visa period, and then apply onshore for a partner visa. A strong option for couples not yet able to evidence a de facto relationship.

## At a glance
- Government charge: From AUD 11,710
- Indicative professional fee: $5,500 AUD (Fixed professional fee quoted after assessment)
- Typical processing: 12–18 months (typical)

## Eligibility
- Both partners 18 or older and free to marry
- Genuine intention to marry within the visa period
- Have met in person and know each other
- Sponsor is an Australian citizen, PR or eligible NZ citizen
- Offshore at application and decision

## Key documents
- Identity documents
- Evidence of relationship and intention to marry (celebrant letter, venue booking)
- Form 888 declarations
- Police clearances and health exams

## Frequently asked questions
### What happens after we marry on a 300 visa?
After marrying within the 9–15 month validity, you apply onshore for the 820/801 partner visa. A reduced partner application charge applies for 300 holders.
