# Skills in Demand (SID) (subclass 482) — Visa2AU

> Employer-sponsored work visa — the TSS successor. Category: Employer.

The Skills in Demand visa (subclass 482) lets Australian employers sponsor skilled workers where local labour is unavailable. Three streams — Specialist Skills, Core Skills (CSOL occupations) and Labour Agreement (incl. DAMA) — with clearer pathways to permanent residency than its predecessor.

## At a glance
- Government charge: From AUD 3,210 (Core/Specialist, main applicant)
- Indicative professional fee: $3,300 AUD (employee side) (Sponsorship, nomination, SAF levy and application fees itemised at engagement)
- Typical processing: 2–4 months (accredited sponsors faster)

## Eligibility
- Approved Standard Business Sponsor (or labour agreement)
- Occupation on the CSOL (Core stream) or ANZSCO major group 1–3 (Specialist stream)
- At least 1 year of relevant work experience
- Skills assessment where required for the occupation
- English: IELTS 5.0 overall (min 5.0) or equivalent
- Market salary at or above the TSMIT/Core Skills threshold

## Key documents
- Sponsorship and nomination applications
- Employment contract and position description
- Evidence of LMT (labour market testing) where required
- Skills assessment (if applicable)
- Qualifications, CV, references
- English test results

## Frequently asked questions
### What changed when TSS became Skills in Demand?
The 482 was restructured into Specialist, Core and Labour Agreement streams with a single CSOL occupation list, reduced experience requirement (1 year), and a clearer 186 pathway after two years with the sponsor.

### Can a 482 lead to permanent residency?
Yes. After two years of sponsored employment (in the last three), the 186 Temporary Residence Transition stream is available, provided the nomination and salary requirements are met.
