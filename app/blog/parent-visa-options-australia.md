Australia offers several pathways for parents of Australian citizens and permanent residents to join their families. Understanding the differences between these options is crucial for making the right choice.

## Contributory Parent Visa (Subclass 143)

This is the most popular parent visa option due to its faster processing time. However, it requires a significant contribution to the Australian government (currently around $43,600 per parent).

**Processing Time:** Approximately 6-12 years (queue-based)

## Parent Visa (Subclass 103)

This is the non-contributory permanent parent visa. It has a much lower government contribution but significantly longer processing times.

**Processing Time:** Approximately 30+ years (queue-based)

## Sponsored Parent Visa (Subclass 870)

This is a temporary visa that allows parents to stay in Australia for up to 5 years. It does not lead to permanent residency but offers a quicker pathway for family reunification.

**Key Features:**

- Requires approval of Parent Sponsor (subclass 870 sponsorship)
- Can stay 3 or 5 years
- Renewable once for another 5 years
- No work rights

## Contributory Aged Parent Visa (Subclass 864)

For parents who are of pension age and are in Australia, this visa allows them to apply onshore and receive a bridging visa while their application is processed.
